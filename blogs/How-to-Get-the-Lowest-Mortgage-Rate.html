Start Small - Take online courses, read books like "The Intelligent Investor," and follow financial news and blogs.


Educate Yourself - Taxes can eat up a significant portion of your wealth, so you should take advantage of tax-advantaged accounts, such as 401(k)s, IRAs, 529 plans, and health savings accounts. You should also consult a tax professional to plan for estate taxes, capital gains taxes, and income taxes.


Create a Budget -Allocate a small, regular amount for investments and ensure you have an emergency fund.


Diversify Your Portfolio - Spread your investments across different assets to minimize risk, using mutual funds and ETFs.


Investing with minimal savings is not only possible but can be highly rewarding. By starting small, educating yourself, and diversifying your investments, you can build a strong financial future while continuing to grow your business and job. Remember, the key is consistency and patience. Happy investing!
